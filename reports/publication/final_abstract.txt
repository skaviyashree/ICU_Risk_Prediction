TITLE: Explainable Artificial Intelligence for Real-Time In-Hospital Mortality Prediction and Bedside Decision Support in Intensive Care Units

ABSTRACT:
Clinical deterioration in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) requires early identification to support clinical decisions, though predictive modeling on pilot-scale datasets is often challenged by extreme class imbalance and alert fatigue. This study presents a proof-of-concept feasibility study demonstrating the deployment and explainability of an explainable AI-based ICU Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS) using a limited open-access ICU cohort. Using the publicly available MIMIC-IV Clinical Demo dataset comprising 100 ICU patients, we developed a leak-free preprocessing pipeline featuring clinical outlier filtering, scale unification, and multimodal statistical aggregation over nine biological markers. Seven machine learning classifiers and two ensembles were evaluated under Stratified 5-Fold Cross-Validation. To address the severe class imbalance (11.0% mortality) and standard decision threshold limitations, we implemented out-of-fold Youden’s J threshold optimization. An ExtraTrees Classifier was selected as the champion model under a fold-averaged decision threshold of 0.39. On the holdout test set, the champion model achieved a classification accuracy of 0.55, a specificity of 0.5556, a sensitivity of 0.50, and an area under the receiver operating characteristic curve (ROC-AUC) of 0.5556. Bedside interpretability is provided via a localized SHAP explanation engine, deconstructing risk predictions into key physiological factors including potassium variability and heart-rate dynamics. The system is deployed as an interactive Streamlit-based CDSS featuring automated PDF reporting to support bedside decision-making. This study demonstrates the feasibility of deploying XAI-based tools under constrained data regimes, establishing a baseline for future scaling to full-scale clinical datasets.
